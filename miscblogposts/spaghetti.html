College student's spaghetti recipe.
Ingredients (amounts depend on hungriness):
Pot
Bowl
Water
Spaghetti (I prefer thin spaghetti or angel hair)
Microwaveable meatballs
Spaghetti sauce Pepper
Chopsticks

Step 1: Boil water

Pretty simple step, some recipes say to add salt when the water boils but I usually don’t. I’ve found that the amount of salt needed to make a difference in taste is significant and I’d rather not waste salt when the pepper and meatballs are flavorful enough.

Step 2: Cook spaghetti

I like my noodles longer, so I put the spaghetti in the pot with about a fourth sticking out (I have a small pot). Then once the spaghetti starts to cook you should be able to rotate the spaghetti into the pot. They might still be a little stiff but once the noodles cook more they will spread out more. Make sure to barely undercook the spaghetti so it will fully cook when mixed with the sauce.

Step 3: Drain

Self explanatory, make sure to leave a smidge of water.

Step 4: Cook meatballs in microwave, and sauce with noodles

With the stove on low heat, put the spaghetti sauce and pepper over the noodles (it’s ok if the sauce is cold). Afterwards, put however many meatballs you want into the bowl and start microwaving according to package instructions. While the meatballs are heating up, stir the spaghetti and sauce making sure to get all the sauce (especially if the sauce is from the refrigerator).

Step 5: Compile everything

Transfer the spaghetti to the bowl, over the cooked meatballs. And there you go! Very few dishes to clean and uses time pretty effectively.
